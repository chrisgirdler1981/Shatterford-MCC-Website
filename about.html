A brief history of Shatterford MCC

In 1973, four motorcycling friends met at the Park Attwood Hotel near Trimpley, Bewdley. All were keen motorcyclists, their interest was to meet and organise joint rally activities, and travel together or meet at venues, this they did for the 1974 season. It soon became clear that other local friends would like to join in the “club”. In 1975 the “four” decided to inaugurate Shatterford Motorcycle Club, so named since the Hamlet of Shatterford was the nearest local point to the Park Attwood, Bewdley already had a club which concentrated mainly on sports, there was no real venue in Trimpley, and was too far from Stourport-on-Severn, whose name would have been too long, so Shatterford it was, then and you could find it easily on the map. After a short while, the owners of the Park Attwood, decided that business was not any longer viably profitable so decided to close, and became a fine nursing home. A new venue was sought......... We go then to a familiar place in the venue world, known for it's disco and live entertainment featuring granny grabbing......The Nautical William........Since much of the venues' activities were mainly Thursday,Friday & Saturday, with weekday resturant and drinks, a Tuesday evening full of motorcycles might boost profits, the club was offered the Cellar Bar as it's own club room..Bingo... the club prepared the room and Tuesday was Shatterford MCC Night, away from the posh populus of the resturant and lounge, and it's own section of car park the numbers began to increase as more local riders became interested.

Shatterford MCC swelled to be a fine club, It's constitution as a rallyist club always present and this was the main occupation on fairly all weekends, rain, shine and...snow! Wonderfully great and memorable times were had. In 1976 the club had became well known in motorcycling circles, enough so an approach was made from the County Road Safety Officer, John Darlington. In an effort to curb the fairly substantial number of serious road incidents and deaths to the vulnerable 16 and 17 year olds on their fast fizzies and kawasaki 250's etc. the club was asked if it would like to provide some basic training funded by the council to the younger elements, and others of the wyre forest district......So a training scheme was born....... Members of the club undertook instructor training provided jointly by Worcs.C.C. And the highly skilled motorcycle section of West Mercia Constabulary, and initiated training at George Street School, Kidderminster on Saturday mornings. Attendance was good overall, many young riders benefiting from the experiences of real motorcyclists, and a great recruitment tool too. Riders came from all around wyre forest and the North Worcestershire environs too....Namely Stourbridge and Hagley, both areas would become the provider of many notable club members.

The rally elements were always strong, and with a good membership, and funds the club organised it's own rally.... The Sheep Naggers Rally....Staged at a few various venues, the Naggers was always a well attended invite only rally, the 1978 one being held at our own clubhouse and much fun had by all. The kind farmer providing the camping land behind the now named Fenn Green. About the turn of the next decade, the 80's, the rival, if you could call them that, Star Rider, which was a paid-for training group, became more prominent nationally, with the proposed introduction of the now necessary CBT, funding now much reduced from the financially struggling Hereford and Worcester CC. The club was forced to consider some form of merger with Star Rider. The feeling was that we were a Rally Club foremost, so motorcycling was our game, the training could now be left with those who were making it their preferred skill and activity, so back to our original formation.... the Rally etc. the club is proud though, that one of our instructors did indeed become Kidderminsters' best known CBT instructor as a result of all that, and is responsible for many riders now. The well supported club had became a well known entity in the motorcycle world, but was dealt a blow when the owner of the Fenn Green decided to sell since the only well supported evening was indeed the Tuesday MCC, good though it was, it couldn't support the rest of the weeks necessary takings. The Fenn closed in 1987 so a new venue was sought. Tuesday at the Nurses Home Kidderminster General Hospital ! Brilliant. Visit anyone who might have had a fall, then onto the clubroom at the nurses quarters for club nite. The club survived for some time here before the Local Area Health Authority decided that nurses didn't need to have subsidised housing, sold the lot off, which eventually became Hume Street Medical Centre. The club sought yet another venue............. The King Billy, Crane Street, Here the club continued for some years, being a meeting place for those left as members. The large membership of the 70's reducing as those got married, had babies, no more explanation needed. Another concern for the club's membership was indeed the fact that the now CBT, and the keeness of new 'ped riders lasted only until a car licence could be obtained. The club continues. After the demise of the King Billy, a new venue was sought..........The Weary Traveller. Not too far from the old venue of the Nurses Home, The club met now with a lot fewer numbers, but still met. The club was looked after by our landlord Chris, sadly, he did pass away, the future of the 'Traveller being in doubt a new venue was sought.......... The Castle, Park Street..........is now our regular meeting place, and indeed our current home. This happens on the first Tuesday every month from 19:30.
